Common Questions

BC Home Buying — FAQ

Is 2026 a good time to buy a home in BC?

+
Yes — for buyers who are financially ready. The Bank of Canada has cut rates significantly from the 2023 peak, improving affordability. Spring 2026 shows rising demand but not the bidding war frenzy of 2021–2022. More inventory = more negotiating power. If you can qualify today, waiting often costs more.

How much down payment do I need in BC?

+
Minimum 5% for homes under $500,000. For $500K–$999K: 5% on first $500K + 10% on the rest. For $1M+: minimum 20% down required. First-time buyers can use FHSA (up to $40K tax-deductible) + RRSP HBP (up to $60K) = up to $100K toward down payment.

How does the First Home Savings Account (FHSA) work?

+
The FHSA lets first-time buyers contribute up to $8,000/year (max $40,000 lifetime) to a tax-free savings account. Contributions are tax-deductible (like RRSP) and withdrawals for home purchase are tax-free (like TFSA). Combined with the RRSP Home Buyers' Plan ($60K), you can access $100,000 tax-free toward your first home.

What is the BC Property Transfer Tax — and can I avoid it?

+
PTT is 1% on the first $200K + 2% on $200K–$2M + 3% above $2M. First-time buyers get full exemption on homes under $500,000 (saving up to $7,700) and partial exemption up to $525,000. New construction under $1.1M may also qualify for PTT exemption.

Do I pay a realtor fee as a buyer in BC?

+
In most BC transactions, the buyer's agent commission is paid by the seller — so you receive professional representation at no direct cost. This has been standard practice in BC for decades. I'll explain any exceptions clearly before we start working together.

How long does buying a home in BC take?

+
Typically 2–4 months from starting your search to getting the keys. Pre-approval: 1–5 days. Home search: 2–8 weeks. Subject removal period: 7–14 days. Completion (closing): usually 30–60 days after accepted offer. In hot markets like Surrey, the search phase can be faster.

What is subject removal and what happens if I back out?

+
After your offer is accepted, you have 7–14 days to complete due diligence (inspection, financing, strata docs). If you're satisfied, you "remove subjects" and the deal is legally binding. If you can't satisfy a condition — say, financing falls through — you can withdraw and your deposit is returned. Removing subjects without completing due diligence is risky and should never be done under pressure.
