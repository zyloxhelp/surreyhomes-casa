How to Buy a Home in BC — 6 Steps

Buying in BC doesn't have to be stressful. Here's exactly how I guide you from start to finish.

1

Get Pre-Approved

Before searching, get a mortgage pre-approval. It confirms your budget, locks your rate for 90–120 days, and shows sellers you're serious.

2

Hire a Realtor

As your buyer's agent, I provide MLS access, neighbourhood expertise, and negotiation — at no direct cost to you as the buyer.

3

Search & Tour

I'll set up custom MLS alerts and personally tour properties with you, flagging potential issues before you fall in love.

4

Make an Offer

When you find the right home, I'll prepare a competitive offer with subject conditions to protect you fully.

5

Subject Removal

During 7–14 days: complete inspection, finalize financing, review strata docs. Then remove subjects — the deal is firm.

6

Get the Keys!

On completion day, funds transfer, title registers, and you get the keys. I'll be there to celebrate with you.

Common Questions

BC Home Buyer FAQ

How much down payment do I need in BC?

+
Minimum 5% for homes under $500,000. For $500K–$999K: 5% on first $500K + 10% on the rest. For $1M+: minimum 20% down. First-time buyers can use FHSA + RRSP HBP for up to $150,000 combined toward their down payment.

Do I pay a realtor fee as a buyer in BC?

+
In most BC transactions, the buyer's agent commission is paid by the seller. As a buyer, you typically pay no direct realtor fee — I represent your interests at no cost to you.

How long does it take to buy a home in BC?

+
Typically 2–4 months total. Pre-approval: 1–5 days. Search: 2–8 weeks. Subject removal: 7–14 days. Completion: 30–60 days after accepted offer. In competitive markets like Surrey and Vancouver, act faster.

What is subject removal in BC?

+
After an accepted offer, you have 7–14 days to satisfy conditions — financing, inspection, strata docs. If satisfied, remove subjects and the deal is firm. If not, walk away with your deposit returned.

What is the BC mortgage stress test?

+
You must qualify at the higher of your actual rate + 2%, or 5.25%. In 2026, most buyers stress test at 6.2–7%. This means you qualify for roughly 20% less mortgage than your rate alone would suggest — get pre-approved early.

What is the Property Transfer Tax in BC?

+
PTT is 1% on first $200K + 2% on $200K–$2M + 3% above $2M. First-time buyers get full exemption on homes under $500,000 (saving up to $7,700) and partial exemption up to $525,000. New construction under $1.1M may also qualify.
